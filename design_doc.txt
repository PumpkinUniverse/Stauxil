1. Overview
Product description
Stauxil is a lightweight SaaS that helps small businesses receive, verify, manage, respond to, 
and document privacy requests such as access, deletion, and correction requests.
What the product does
 Provides a public request form that customers can submit without signing in.
 Gives the business a private dashboard for reviewing and processing requests.
 Uses email-based verification so teams can confirm the requester is legitimate.
 Tracks deadlines, ownership, notes, emails, and a full activity trail.
 Supports basic exports so the business can retain evidence of what happened in each 
case.
Plain-English framing
Stauxil is a privacy request inbox for small businesses. It is a workflow and documentation 
layer, not a law firm, not a legal decision engine, and not an automatic delete-everything 
service.
2. Problem Statement
Small businesses increasingly need to handle privacy-related requests from customers, users,
and clients, but most still process them through shared inboxes, spreadsheets, and ad hoc 
notes. That leads to missed deadlines, inconsistent verification, fragmented internal 
handling, and weak proof that the business responded appropriately.
 Requests arrive through multiple channels and are easy to miss.
 Owners do not always know who should handle a request next.
 Internal notes and customer-facing replies become scattered across email threads.
 Teams lack a case-level audit trail if they need to show what happened later.
 Enterprise privacy tools are often too expensive and too heavy for smaller teams.
3. Goals and Success Criteria
Primary goals
1. Let an external requester submit a privacy request through a branded public page.
2. Let the business verify the requester through email.
3. Let the business manage requests in a private dashboard.
4. Let the business send templated responses and keep a full activity trail.
5. Let the business export case history.

4. Non-Goals for the MVP
The MVP intentionally avoids broad compliance automation. The first version should be a 
reliable workflow tool, not a deep integration product or a legal expert system.
 No automatic discovery of personal data across third-party systems.
 No automatic deletion in external services.
 No legal advice or legal conclusions.
 No document or selfie identity verification.
 No SSO, advanced RBAC, or enterprise procurement features.
 No deep integrations with Stripe, HubSpot, Intercom, Gmail, or support tools in v1.
 No AI-generated legal policy drafting.

7. Functional Requirements
7.1 Public intake form
 Public route supports unauthenticated request submission.
 Required fields: full name, email, request type, jurisdiction or region, request details.
 Optional field: account or customer reference.
 Workspace-configurable branding: company name, logo, intro copy, success message, 
allowed request types.
7.2 Verification
 Support email-based verification with either a magic link or a one-time code.
 Store verification state independently from request workflow status.
 Allow manual override by an internal user for edge cases.
7.3 Dashboard
 Display case ID, requester, request type, status, owner, created date, due date, and time 
remaining.
 Filters: status, owner, type, overdue only, and free-text search by case ID or requester 
email.
 Top-of-page summary cards: open requests, overdue requests, due this week, fulfilled this
month.

7.4 Request detail page
 Show requester info, request summary, verification state, due date, internal notes, 
timeline, email history, and attachments.
 Actions: assign owner, update status, send verification email, add note, send email from 
template, mark complete, close case.
7.5 Deadline tracking
 Every request gets a due date based on workspace default SLA.
 Requests show due soon and overdue states clearly in the UI.
 Reminder emails should be triggered before and after the due date.
7.6 Templates and exports
 Support editable email templates for verification, acknowledgement, more-info-needed, 
completion, and denial.
 Provide CSV export of requests and a printable single-case summary.
8. Legal and Positioning Constraints
Stauxil must be presented as a workflow tool that helps teams manage privacy requests. The 
product should help with intake, verification, tracking, communication, and evidence. It 
should not promise legal outcomes or guaranteed compliance.

17. Security and Access Control
 Public submission route should be rate-limited or bot-protected.
 Verification tokens must be hashed at rest.
 Every authenticated query and mutation must validate workspace membership server￾side.
 Never trust client-submitted workspace ownership or cross-workspace request IDs.
 Do not expose all requests across workspaces from a shared query.

20. UX Notes
 The dashboard should feel like an inbox: fast scanning, obvious status, minimal clutter.
 Trust and clarity matter more than decorative styling.
 Overdue warnings must be unmistakable without creating constant red-noise across the 
page.
 When there are no requests, the product should explain the workflow and point the 
owner to their public intake page.

26. Non-Technical Summary
Stauxil gives a company a branded form where customers can submit privacy requests, and 
a private workspace where the company can manage those requests from start to finish. It 
verifies the requester, tracks deadlines, stores notes and actions, helps teams send consistent 
replies, and preserves a case history so nothing gets lost.
